A peek into my mind...

I'm passionate about building innovative things that matter to the world

In the past year and a half, I've dived into the tech world, dreaming of making a difference out there. I've taught myself tools of the trade such as Ruby on Rails and Node.js, in order to turn project ideas into realities. Right now, I work with a FinTech startup that aims to replace the financial advisor to a financial online mentor. At the same time, I am a full-time student at NYU, where I participate in an academic project, whose ultimate goal is to uncover the Deep Web. For me what matters is to be part of something that can shape the world we live in and impact the lives of people.

I was born in Brazil, where I grew up and went to the best university in Latin America, according to QS University Rankings 2015. While my time as a full-time Electrical Engineering student, I got fairly acquainted to the financial markets, doing several intensive courses and a summer internship at a brokerage firm in Sao Paulo, assisting the team that was branching out the firm to New York. On my last year at school, I was selected among 60 people to work as a finance intern for Procter & Gamble. However, I quickly discovered that I didn't have flexibility to reach my full potencial. I was growing bored and unfulfilled.

After a few months, I started reassessing what my options were. I knew I had great quantitative skills but nowhere to apply them in Brazil. At least not in the short term. I was also fed up with the status quo. I needed to break it. I needed to get out of my comfort zone. So, I decided to move out of the country. But where to go? Then, I learned about the Start Up boom going on in the US. I was fascinated with the idea of being part of the technology vanguard of the world. Going to a good graduate school there would give me a competitive edge to find a meaninful opportunity. I applied to a Masters at NYU and got a reasonable scholarship offer. That was it. I took the leap and came to New York!
